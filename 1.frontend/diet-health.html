ü©∫ 1. Chronic & Common Diseases

Examples: Cough, Cold, Asthma, Diabetes, Hypertension, Arthritis

Promotes Awareness

  • Learn how chronic diseases develop ‚Äî often due to lifestyle, pollution, or heredity.
  • Know early symptoms like persistent cough, fatigue, or high blood pressure.
  • Attend health checkups and understand the importance of regular monitoring.

Encourages Healthy Habits

  • Eat balanced meals rich in fiber, fruits, and vegetables.
  • Avoid excessive sugar, salt, and oily foods.
  • Exercise regularly (walking, yoga, breathing exercises).
  • Take medicines as prescribed and avoid self-medication.

Prevention over Cure

  • Get vaccinated against influenza and pneumonia (for elderly).
  • Maintain good indoor air quality ‚Äî avoid smoking or dust exposure.
  • Manage stress through relaxation and meditation.
  • Get blood pressure and sugar levels checked periodically.

Empowers Communities

  • Encourage others to quit smoking or tobacco.
  • Promote air cleanliness in your neighborhood.
  • Support local health camps for diabetes or heart screening.

Improves Quality of Life

  • Keep a daily health diary for medication and activity.
  • Balance work, rest, and recreation.
  • Stay socially connected ‚Äî isolation can worsen chronic illness.

ü§¢ 2. Communicable & Infectious Diseases

Examples: COVID-19, Influenza, Tuberculosis, Malaria, Dengue, Typhoid

Promotes Awareness

  • Learn how infections spread ‚Äî through air, water, or contact.
  • Understand symptoms like fever, cough, rashes, or fatigue.
  • Know the importance of early testing and quarantine if necessary.

Encourages Healthy Habits

  • Wash hands frequently with soap or sanitizer.
  • Use clean water and eat freshly cooked food.
  • Wear masks in crowded areas.
  • Avoid close contact with infected individuals.

Prevention over Cure

  • Get vaccinated for diseases like COVID-19, Hepatitis B, etc.
  • Use mosquito nets and repellents to prevent malaria and dengue.
  • Maintain personal hygiene and environmental cleanliness.
  • Dispose of waste properly and cover food.

Empowers Communities

  • Participate in community cleaning and awareness programs.
  • Support public vaccination drives.
  • Report disease outbreaks to local health centers.

Improves Quality of Life

  • Keep surroundings clean and ventilated.
  • Maintain a healthy immune system through proper diet and sleep.
  • Avoid panic ‚Äî follow medical advice calmly.

üçΩÔ∏è 3. Nutritional & Lifestyle Diseases

Examples: Obesity, Diabetes, Heart Disease, Anemia

Promotes Awareness

  • Learn about the role of nutrition and physical activity in health.
  • Know how poor diet and inactivity can cause obesity or diabetes.

Encourages Healthy Habits

  • Eat balanced meals: fruits, vegetables, proteins, whole grains.
  • Avoid processed and fast foods.
  • Drink plenty of water.
  • Exercise at least 30 minutes daily.

Prevention over Cure

  • Go for regular BMI, cholesterol, and sugar checkups.
  • Limit sugar, oil, and salt intake.
  • Follow diet plans recommended by doctors or dietitians.

Empowers Communities

  • Join fitness or diet awareness groups.
  • Promote healthy eating habits in schools and workplaces.
  • Organize group walks or sports activities.

Improves Quality of Life

  • Maintain a healthy weight.
  • Get proper rest and reduce stress.
  • Enjoy outdoor activities and balanced routines.

üß† 4. Mental Health Issues

Examples: Depression, Anxiety, Stress Disorders

Promotes Awareness

  • Learn to recognize signs of mental stress or depression.
  • Understand that mental health is as important as physical health.

Encourages Healthy Habits

  • Maintain a consistent sleep schedule.
  • Talk openly with trusted friends or counselors.
  • Practice meditation or mindfulness.

Prevention over Cure

  • Avoid overworking and learn to manage time effectively.
  • Limit screen time and social media pressure.
  • Engage in hobbies and positive social activities.

Empowers Communities

  • Create support groups in schools, colleges, and workplaces.
  • Encourage open discussions about mental health without stigma.

Improves Quality of Life

  • Build strong emotional relationships.
  • Take breaks when stressed.
  • Stay optimistic and practice gratitude.

üß´ 5. Environmental & Sanitation-Related Diseases

Examples: Cholera, Typhoid, Jaundice, Skin Infections

Promotes Awareness

  • Understand that many diseases come from poor sanitation and unsafe water.
  • Know the importance of clean surroundings and waste disposal.

Encourages Healthy Habits

  • Always use clean, safe drinking water.
  • Wash fruits and vegetables before eating.
  • Maintain toilet hygiene and personal cleanliness.

Prevention over Cure

  • Use water filters or boil water before drinking.
  • Dispose garbage properly to avoid pests.
  • Get vaccinated for hepatitis and typhoid.

Empowers Communities

  • Support clean-up campaigns in your area.
  • Promote construction and use of proper toilets.
  • Report leaking pipelines or unclean water sources.

Improves Quality of Life

  • Keep your home and surroundings neat.
  • Enjoy disease-free, fresh environments.
  • Encourage others to adopt the same hygiene standards.
